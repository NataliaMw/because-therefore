### 0:00 – 0:20 · The hook  *(landing screen)*
> "Everyone says AI killed critical thinking. So I built a game that makes you do the one thing AI can't do for you — figure out what actually caused what. This is Because, Therefore. You get a real disaster as scrambled cause-and-effect cards, and you race to rebuild the true chain."

### 0:20 – 0:50 · Start a round  *(click Quick Play / Solo, load the Molasses Flood)*
[click into a round — the Boston Molasses Flood]
> "1919, Boston. A tank of molasses bursts and floods the streets. Here are the pieces — out of order. Wartime demand, a rushed tank, warnings everyone ignored, the rupture, the wave, the lawsuit. My job: put them in true causal order, not just the order they sound good in."
[drag/arrange the cards into the chain]

### 0:50 – 1:20 · The trap  *(point out the correlation-not-causation card)*
> "And there's a trap. One card correlates — it happened around the same time — but it did NOT cause anything. Include it in the chain and I lose points. That's the whole lesson: correlation is not causation, and the game punishes you for confusing them."
[leave the trap card OUT of the chain]

### 1:20 – 1:50 · The aha reveal  *(submit → watch the reveal)*
[click submit / reveal]
> "Submit — and the true chain snaps into place, each link explaining WHY it caused the next. That 'oh, THAT'S why' moment is the point. You don't memorize the molasses flood; you reason your way through it."
[the score + correct chain animate in]

### 1:50 – 2:20 · Make your own + GPT-5.6  *(click "make your own round", type a topic)*
> "And it works on anything. Type any topic —"
[type e.g. "the fall of Rome" and generate]
> "— and GPT-5.6 turns it into a fresh scrambled causal chain with its own hidden trap card. That generation is the AI engine of the game. It also runs fully offline with built-in rounds, so a classroom with no internet still plays."

### 2:20 – 2:40 · How it's built + close
> "I used GPT-5.6 to design the round-generation — turning any topic into a causal chain with a plausible-but-wrong trap — and I used Codex to build the whole game: the server, the scoring, the reveal, all zero-dependency and offline-first. Because, Therefore — the game that makes you earn the 'because.'"
